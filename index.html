Marina Bay Sands Expo & Convention Center, Singapore - 16-19 Oct 2023

IES Industry Days Program

The IEEE Industrial Electronics Society (IES) is establishing a new industry valued event series co-located with their flagship research conference IEEE IECON 2023 in Singapore at Marina Bay Sands. Like IECON, it will repeat each year. The IES Industry Days program, similarly to other international industry events, features:

  • Exhibition Hall
  • Industry Executive Keynotes
  • Technology Training for Engineers and Leaders
  • Technology Directions for Corporate Leaders: Impact on Business

We bring together Technical Experts and Industry to advance Industrial technologies and products. IES technologies span all those for industrial solutions including sensors, robotics, control systems, automation, and power along with expertise in the needed infrastructure including networking, informatics, processing, and standards.

Audience: Industry engineers, leaders in Information and Communication Technologies (ICT), Operations, and Automation/Manufacturing.

Why exhibit at IES Industry Days:

  • Major opportunity for new customers engaging with a broad network of customers and suppliers
  • Training opportunities for engineers and corporate leadership in the latest technologies and their use in Industrial applications.
  • Recruitment: Access high-caliber graduates participating in IECON.

Locations: Each year will showcase in a different region and open opportunities for customer engagements.

  • October 2023: Singapore, Republic of Singapore (co-located with IEEE IECON 2023)
  • October 2024: Chicago, USA (co-located with IEEE IECON 2024)
  • October 2025: Madrid, Spain (co-located with IEEE IECON 2025)

In addition to IEEE IECON’s 2,500 technical experts, researchers, and graduate attendees, Industry Days will look to attract practitioners and executives across the industry engaged in industrial electronics products. Including Operations, Information, and Manufacturing.

Topics

Industry Day focuses on several technology topics in the domains of Energy and Sensors & Control.

Technology: Energy

  • Electrical Machines
  • Energy Storage
  • Power Electronics
  • Renewable Energy Systems
  • Smart Grids
  • Transportation Electrification

Technology: Sensors & Control

  • Control, Robotics, and Mechatronics
  • Data-Driven Control and Monitoring
  • MEMS and Nanotechnologies
  • Motion Control
  • Network-based Control Systems and Applications
  • Sensors and Actuators

Technology: ICT

  • Building Automation, Control, and Management
  • Cloud and Wireless Systems for Industrial Applications
  • Factory Automation
  • Industrial Agents
  • Industrial Cyber-Physical Systems
  • Industrial Informatics

Technology: Cross-Disciplinary

  • Education in Engineering and Industrial Technologies
  • Electronic Systems on Chip
  • Human Factors
  • Resilience and Security for Industrial Applications
  • Standards

In addition to the exhibition hall, the new Industry Days program will include thought leadership focused on the business potential of emerging technology. The complete program will be announced Spring 2023.

Vision Sessions: Business Impacts of Technology

  • Building Innovation: Skills needed to build new innovations in products and services, useful both in an existing company or startup.
  • Managing Finances And Attracting Capital: Financial management skills for a successful startup.
  • Digital Twin Technologies: Impact on industrial business strategy including component providers and system designers.
  • Artificial Intelligence: Ethics, challenges and benefits in the use of AI in industrial applications.

Technology Training

  • Smart Cities Technology
  • Battery Management
  • Battery System Design
  • Smart Sensors
  • Emerging Technologies In Industrial Automation
